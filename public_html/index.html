Bicycle Tours in Stockholm

The Guided Stockholm Tour

Stockholm Cykel will guide you on a 15 kilometer bike tour through beautiful and exciting areas of Stockholm outside the common tourist routes. You will bike the narrow streets and beaches of Söder and other parts of Stockholm. From the bike seat we will discover the town in an easy pace. You will bike in a small group together with a local guide and we will do many stops when reaching interesting spots. The tour duration is 2-3 hours and the cost is 350 SEK.

Our bikes are modern multi-speed city bicycles. If you prefer to use your own bike, the cost is 250 SEK.

15 km 2-3 hours Brännkyrkagatan, Södermalm 350 SEK Thursdays 5pm (April to August), Saturday 10am (April, September).

Drottningholm Castle - Self-guided tour

Meet Lake Mälaren and Drottningholm Castle on two wheels!

On this self-guided day tour, you will bike through the changing landscape of the shores of Lake Mälaren. This is an easy tour on bike lanes or low traffic streets containing very few uphill sections.

You will meet a diversity of Swedish architecture, all from historical buildings to modern suburban areas. You may also observe some ancient monuments such as milestones.

On the tour you will pass Drottningholm Castle, the home of the royal family of Sweden. In Vinterviken you may take a coffee break in the old factory of the industrialist Alfred Nobel, the founder of the Nobel Prize. Tantolunden with its picturesqe allotment gardens is also a nice place for a break.

You will pass several nice outdoor baths and beaches so do not forget your swim suite!

    Skogskyrkogården - Self-guided tour

    World heritage Woodland Cemetery and Nacka nature reserve

    On this self-guided day tour, you will bike through woodlands and on the shores of small lakes and the Baltic Sea. This is an easy tour on bike lanes or low traffic streets with very few uphill sections.

    On this tour you will pass the world heritage Skogskyrkogården (the Woodland Cemetery), created by the architects Asplund and Lewerentz between 1917 and 1940. The tour continues through the Nacka nature reserve and to the Baltic sea inlet of Stockholm.

    You will see industrial areas such as shipyards and mills of Stockholm, currently transforming into modern contemporary housing with excellent sea view.

    You will also pass several nice open-air baths and beaches so do not forget your swim suite!
